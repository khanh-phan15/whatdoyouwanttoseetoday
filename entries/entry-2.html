TUỒNG - VIETNAMESE CLASSICAL THEATRE

Tuồng (Vietnamese classical theater) originated in central Vietnam during the 12th century, influenced by Chinese opera. Initially performed in royal courts, it later spread to the general public. Tuồng combines music, acting, and dance to tell historical, mythical, and moral stories, often focusing on themes of loyalty, patriotism, and heroism. Performers use exaggerated gestures, stylized makeup, and elaborate costumes to portray characters. Over time, tuồng became a popular theatrical form in Vietnam, blending both traditional and modern elements.

What stood out to me was how every detail, from the makeup to the choreography, is so steeped in tradition. It’s like stepping back in time and getting a glimpse of Vietnam’s cultural roots. Watching tuồng isn’t just entertainment—it’s an experience that leaves you in awe of the dedication and artistry behind it.
