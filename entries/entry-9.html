HÁT XẨM - XAM SINGING

Hát Xẩm (Xẩm singing) has roots in Vietnam's northern regions, dating back several centuries, though its exact origin remains uncertain. Traditionally, it was performed by blind musicians, often in busy marketplaces, on street corners, or during local festivals. These performers used hát xẩm both to entertain and to earn a living, relying on the generosity of passersby. The lyrics of hát xẩm typically address themes of hardship, love, humor, and social commentary, making it a voice for the common people. During the early 20th century, hát xẩm thrived, but as new forms of entertainment emerged and social structures changed, it began to decline. Efforts to revive hát xẩm began in the late 20th century, and today it’s recognized as an important part of Vietnam's cultural heritage, with musicians and cultural organizations working to preserve and promote this unique art form.
